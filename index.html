Enhancing Digital Accessibility in My Bootcamp Project

A WCAG-informed review of my final Makers bootcamp project, focusing on accessibility improvements.

What Worked Well

During my review, I identified several strengths in the project's accessibility:

  • Strong Color Contrast: The main content met WCAG 2.1 Success Criterion 1.4.3 (Contrast Minimum), ensuring readability for users with visual impairments.
  • Logical Navigation Flow: The intuitive navigation supported WCAG 2.1 Principle 2 (Operable), making it easier for users to interact with the content.
  • Properly Labelled Links: Most links were descriptive, meeting WCAG 2.1 Success Criterion 2.4.4 (Link Purpose in Context).

Areas for Improvement

I also identified key areas that needed enhancement to meet WCAG standards:

  • Descriptive Page Titles: The page title needed to be more descriptive to comply with WCAG 2.1 Success Criterion 2.4.2 (Page Titled).
  • Missing ARIA Labels: Adding ARIA labels for images and interactive elements would meet WCAG 2.1 Success Criterion 1.1.1 (Non-text Content).
  • Improved Heading Structure: Refining the heading structure aligned with WCAG 2.1 Success Criterion 1.3.1 (Info and Relationships).
  • Clearer Visual Focus Indicators: Enhancing focus indicators supported WCAG 2.1 Success Criterion 2.4.7 (Focus Visible).
  • Responsive Design Tweaks: Adjustments ensured the design remained usable at 250%+ zoom, meeting WCAG 2.1 Success Criterion 1.4.10 (Reflow).

Actions Taken

Instead of trying to take on a complete overhaul I kept it focused and targeted the most impactful improvements

  • Simplified Navigation: Removed redundant dropdown menus to streamline navigation, aligning with WCAG 2.1 Principle 2 (Operable).
  • Improved Form Accessibility: Added aria-describedby and aria-live attributes to forms, complying with WCAG 2.1 Success Criterion 3.3.1 (Error Identification).
  • Color Contrast Updates: Adjusted the red accent color to meet WCAG 2.1 Success Criterion 1.4.3 (Contrast Minimum).
  • Testing with Assistive Technology: Used NVDA to identify and clean up over-labeled elements and unnecessary HTML attributes, aligning with WCAG 2.1 Principle 1 (Perceivable).

Key Takeaways

This process reinforced several important lessons:

  • Accessibility is Integral: Building accessibility into the design process from the start saves time and creates a better experience for all users.
  • Testing is Crucial: Testing with assistive technology revealed practical issues that automated tools might miss.
  • Small Changes, Big Impact: Simple updates, like improving page titles and adding ARIA labels, significantly enhanced accessibility.

By applying WCAG principles and success criteria, I was able to make my project more inclusive and user-friendly. This experience reinforced the importance of accessibility in digital design and inspired me to continue building inclusive experiences.
